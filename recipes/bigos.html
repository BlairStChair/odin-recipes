Polish Hunter's Stew

Describtion

Bigos, known as Polish hunter's stew, is a hearty dish made with meats like Polish sausage
and beef stew meat, cooked with sauerkraut, cabbage, mushrooms, and spices.
It's simmered slowly for rich, complex flavors, making it a beloved comfort food in Polish cuisine,
enjoyed during gatherings and festivities.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Polish sausage (kielbasa), sliced
  • 1 lb beef stew meat, cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sauerkraut, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup chopped mushrooms
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Brown the Meats:

    In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the sliced sausage and cubed beef stew meat over medium heat
    until nicely browned. Remove from the pot and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables:

    In the same pot, add chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is soft and translucent.
    Add chopped mushrooms and shredded cabbage to the pot. Cook for a few minutes until the mushrooms are softened.

  3. Combine Ingredients:

    Return the browned meats to the pot. Add drained sauerkraut, diced tomatoes (with their juices),
    beef broth, bay leaf, dried thyme, and paprika. Stir to combine.

  4. Simmer:

    Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium-low heat. Cover and let it simmer for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
    The longer it simmers, the more the flavors will develop. You can simmer for up to 2-3 hours for even richer flavor.

  5. Season:

    Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Remove the bay leaf before serving.

  6. Serve:

    Serve hot, either on its own or with crusty bread or boiled potatoes. Bigos is often even better the next day,
    as the flavors have more time to meld together.
