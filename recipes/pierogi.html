Pierogi Recipe

Describtion

Experience the comfort of homemade pierogi with our authentic recipe.
These Polish dumplings feature tender dough filled with savory goodness,
boiled to perfection.
Serve hot with your favorite toppings for a delightful culinary journey.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes (you can also add cheese, onions, or other fillings if desired)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: sautéed onions, sour cream, chopped bacon

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, sour cream, and softened butter until well combined.
    Gradually add the wet ingredients to the flour mixture, stirring until a dough forms. Knead the dough
    on a floured surface for about 5 minutes, until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    Mix mashed potatoes with any desired fillings such as cheese, onions, or seasonings. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Assemble the Pierogi:

    Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness. Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out circles of dough.
    Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape,
    then pinch the edges firmly to seal. You can use a fork to crimp the edges for extra security.

  4. Cook the Pierogi:

    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the pierogi into the boiling water, working in batches to avoid overcrowding.
    Boil the pierogi for about 3-5 minutes, or until they float to the surface. Remove the pierogi with a slotted spoon and transfer them to a plate.

  5. Optional: Fry the Pierogi (for extra flavor):

    In a skillet, melt some butter over medium heat. Add the boiled pierogi to the skillet and fry until golden brown on both sides,
    about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from the skillet and drain on paper towels.

  6. Serve:

    Serve the pierogi hot, topped with sautéed onions, a dollop of sour cream, and chopped bacon if desired. Enjoy your delicious homemade pierogi!
