Polish Hunter's Stew

Describtion

Zurek is a classic Polish sour rye soup, known for its tangy taste and hearty ingredients
like sourdough bread, sausage, and mushrooms. It's a beloved comfort food often enjoyed
during festivities in Poland.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup sourdough bread, diced or torn into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup cooked and diced Polish sausage (such as kielbasa) or bacon (optional)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried marjoram
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Hard-boiled eggs, sliced (for serving)
  • Chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Broth Base:

    In a large pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a simmer over medium heat.

  2. Prepare the Sourdough Base:

    Place the diced sourdough bread in a bowl and pour a ladleful of hot broth over it.
    Let it soak for about 10 minutes.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics:

    In a skillet, melt the butter or heat the oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic,
    and cook until softened. Add sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their juices and start to brown.

  4. Combine Ingredients:

    Add the sautéed aromatics to the pot of simmering broth. Stir in the soaked sourdough bread
    and cooked Polish sausage or bacon (if using). Add bay leaves and dried marjoram. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Thicken the Soup:

    In a small bowl, mix sour cream with flour until smooth. Gradually whisk in water until well combined.
    Gradually pour the sour cream mixture into the soup, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Cook for another 5-10 minutes
    until the soup thickens slightly.

  6. Serve:

    Ladle the zurek soup into bowls. Garnish with slices of hard-boiled egg and chopped fresh parsley.
    Serve hot and enjoy the tangy and comforting flavors of this traditional Polish soup!
